Frequently Asked Questions

Source: Unsplash

How do I know if my child needs therapy?

If your child is struggling with their emotions, behavior, or relationships in a way that is affecting their daily life, school performance, or overall happiness, it may be worth speaking with a professional. Some signs to look out for include persistent sadness or irritability, increased anxiety or worry, withdrawal from friends and activities, changes in sleep or appetite, and declining grades. If you are unsure, reaching out for an initial consultation is always a good first step.

How does the first session work?

The first session is typically a consultation where Dr. Altman meets with the parents to better understand their concerns and learn about their child's history and current challenges. Depending on the age of the child, Dr. Altman may also meet with the child directly during this first visit. This session helps Dr. Altman determine the best path forward and gives families the opportunity to ask questions and get a feel for whether the practice is the right fit.

How long does therapy take?

The length of therapy varies depending on the individual and the nature of their challenges. Some clients benefit from a shorter course of treatment over a few months, while others find ongoing therapy helpful over a longer period. Dr. Altman regularly checks in with clients and families to assess progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed.

Do you accept insurance?

Dr. Altman is an out-of-network provider. This means that while she does not bill insurance directly, many families are able to seek reimbursement from their insurance provider for a portion of the session fees. Dr. Altman is happy to provide documentation to support insurance claims upon request.

What are your session fees?

Initial consultation fees and ongoing session rates are available upon request. Please reach out via the contact page to inquire about current fees.

Where is the practice located?

Dr. Altman's practice is located on the Upper West Side of Manhattan, New York City. Additional details about the office location are available on the contact page.

Is therapy confidential?

Yes. Everything discussed in therapy is kept strictly confidential. There are a small number of legal exceptions to confidentiality, such as situations where there is a risk of harm to the client or others, which Dr. Altman will explain in detail at the start of treatment.

How do I get started?

Getting started is simple. Reach out via the contact page or send an email to schedule an initial consultation. Dr. Altman typically responds within one to two business days.
