About Dr. Altman

Dr. Bonnie Altman is a licensed psychologist based in New York City with over 21 years of experience working with children, adolescents, and families. She earned her B.S. in Human Development from Cornell University in 1996 and her Psy.D. from Pace University.

For the past two decades, Bonnie has served as a supervising psychologist at a school on the Upper West Side of Manhattan, supporting students from nursery through 12th grade. Her work spans one-on-one counseling with students, close collaboration with teachers, and providing guidance to families navigating difficult moments. Teenagers are where her heart is, and she has spent the majority of her career helping young people manage anxiety, depression, stress, and the everyday challenges of growing up.

Outside of her clinical work, Bonnie has given numerous talks on mental health, student well-being, and stress management, and has mentored many early-career psychologists. She brings the same warmth, curiosity, and care to every session that she has brought to her work for over two decades.

The Office

Dr. Altman's office is designed to feel warm, calm, and welcoming. Located on the Upper West Side of Manhattan, the space is thoughtfully designed to help clients feel at ease from the moment they walk in. The office features comfortable seating, soft lighting, and a quiet, private atmosphere that allows for open and honest conversation. Every detail has been carefully considered to create an environment that feels safe, comfortable, and conducive to the therapeutic process. Parents and children alike consistently remark on how relaxed and at home they feel in the space.
